# Mini-Tournament — AI Assessment Landing Page

**Context:** Standalone landing page for the paid discovery offer ("AI Assessment"). Sent directly to warm leads (LinkedIn, post-call follow-ups). The visitor is deciding whether to spend money — CFO and Ideal Customer judges carry the most weight here.

**Learnings applied from Tournament #1:**
- Ownership frame won → at least one version must extend it
- CFO demands concrete deliverables and numbers → every version must list exact outputs
- Direct V7 voice → baseline tone for all versions
- Generic consultancy structure (V4) lost badly → no version repeats it

**Format:** 4 targeted versions instead of 7 — the hypothesis space is already narrowed.

---

## PART 1 — THE 4 CONTENDERS

### A — "De-Risk Before You Commit" (risk-first, CFO-targeted)

**Hero:**
> **Find out if AI is worth it for your firm — before you spend real money on it.**
> A fixed-price, fixed-scope assessment. Two weeks. You end up with a deployment plan you own — whether we build it together or not.

**Structure:** The expensive mistake (jumping into AI projects blind) → What the assessment prevents → What you get → Price & timeline → Book.

**Key blocks:**
- "Most failed AI projects fail before the first line of code — wrong use case, wrong data, wrong expectations. The assessment is two weeks of finding that out for a fixed price, instead of six months of finding it out in production."
- "Worst case: you spend [X] and learn AI isn't ready for your workflow yet. That's the cheapest 'no' you'll ever buy."

**Bet:** Frame the assessment as insurance against a much larger loss.

---

### B — "The Plan Is Yours to Keep" (ownership frame extended)

**Hero:**
> **A deployment plan you own. Whether you hire me or not.**
> Two weeks. Fixed price. At the end you hold a document your team — or any competent vendor — can execute.

**Structure:** The promise (no lock-in, even at discovery stage) → What's inside the document → Process week by week → Price → Book.

**Key blocks:**
- "Consultants sell dependency. This assessment is designed to make you independent: use case validation, data audit, infrastructure spec, cost projection — written so anyone can act on it."
- "If the plan says 'don't do this,' it will say so. I'd rather lose a build project than deliver one that shouldn't exist."

**Bet:** Extending the site's master frame builds trust through consistency — the visitor already read "systems you own" on Home.

---

### C — "Diagnosis Before Prescription" (expertise/method-first)

**Hero:**
> **You wouldn't accept surgery without a diagnosis. Don't accept an AI proposal without one either.**

**Structure:** The medical analogy → The three questions the assessment answers (Is your data usable? Which use case pays first? What does it cost to run?) → Method → Deliverable → Book.

**Key blocks:**
- "Three questions, answered with evidence: 1) Is your data actually usable — quality, structure, volume? 2) Which use case pays back first? 3) What does sovereign deployment really cost — hardware, hosting, maintenance?"
- Method: document sampling → workflow interviews → infrastructure review → written plan.

**Bet:** The analogy makes the paid-discovery concept instantly legible to non-technical buyers.

---

### D — "What You'll Know in 14 Days" (outcome/timeline-first)

**Hero:**
> **In 14 days you'll know exactly what AI can do for your firm — with numbers.**
> Not a sales pitch shaped like a report. An engineering assessment with a price tag on every recommendation.

**Structure:** Day-by-day timeline (Days 1–3: data audit → Days 4–8: use case validation with a working proof-of-concept search over your sample documents → Days 9–14: plan & costing) → Deliverables list → Price → Book.

**Key blocks:**
- "By day 8 you'll have seen a working search over a sample of your own documents — not a demo with my data, a test with yours."
- Deliverables: use-case ranking, data readiness report, infrastructure spec (on-prem vs EU-hosted), 12-month TCO projection, go/no-go recommendation.

**Bet:** Concreteness of the timeline + the mid-assessment proof-of-concept is the strongest possible evidence.

**Why D wins:** the mid-assessment proof-of-concept ("working search over your documents by day 8") scored 9 with both the Customer and the Competitor — it's simultaneously the strongest buying trigger and the hardest element to copy, because it depends on infrastructure you actually own. The merge strategy: **D's skeleton** + **A's risk language** (hero edge + "cheapest no" close) + **B's independence clause** + **C's three questions** as the intro device.

## PART 4 — FINAL PAGE: /ai-assessment

**Working title: "AI Assessment — 14 Days to a Plan You Own"**

---

**Hero:**
> **In 14 days, you'll know exactly what AI can do for your firm — before you spend real money on it.**
> A fixed-price engineering assessment. By day 8 you'll see a working search over a sample of your own documents. By day 14 you'll hold a deployment plan you own — whether we build it together or not.
>
> [Book the assessment] · Fixed price: [X] € · Remote or on-site (EU)

**Section 1 — Three questions, answered with evidence** *(from C)*
> 1. **Is your data actually usable?** Quality, structure, volume — measured, not assumed.
> 2. **Which use case pays back first?** Ranked by effort vs. impact, with your team's input.
> 3. **What does sovereign AI really cost to run?** Hardware, hosting, maintenance — a 12-month projection, not a guess.

**Section 2 — The 14 days** *(D's skeleton)*
> **Days 1–3 — Data audit.** I sample your documents and workflows. You learn immediately if there's a structural blocker.
> **Days 4–8 — Proof of concept.** A working search over a sample of your own documents, on private infrastructure. Not a demo with my data — a test with yours.
> **Days 9–14 — The plan.** Use-case ranking, data readiness report, infrastructure spec (on-premise vs. EU-hosted private server), 12-month total cost of ownership, and a clear go/no-go recommendation.

**Section 3 — The document is yours** *(B's independence clause)*
> The plan is written so that your team — or any competent vendor — can execute it. If the honest answer is "AI isn't ready for your workflow yet," the plan will say so. I'd rather lose a build project than deliver one that shouldn't exist.

**Section 4 — Why fixed price** *(A's risk language)*
> Most failed AI projects fail before the first line of code — wrong use case, wrong data, wrong expectations. The assessment is two weeks of finding that out for a fixed price, instead of six months of finding it out in production.
> Worst case: you spend [X] € and learn AI isn't ready for your firm yet. **That's the cheapest "no" you'll ever buy.**

**Section 5 — Who this is for**
> Law firms, medical practices, engineering bureaus, and any EU organization where documents are confidential by law or by contract. If your compliance officer winces at "cloud AI," this assessment was designed for you.

**CTA:**
> **One call to start.** 30 minutes, no pitch. We check fit, pick a sample of documents, and schedule day 1.
> [Book the call] · [Email]
