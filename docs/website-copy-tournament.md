# Website Copy Tournament — radosavbrdar.com

**Goal:** Validation-after-referral site. English-first, 5–6 pages (Home, Services, Work, About, Contact) + service "products."
**Method:** 7 versions, each with a different hook and structure → 5 judges score each → losers eliminated → best elements merged into a final version.

---

## PART 1 — THE 7 CONTENDERS

Each version = hero copy + page structure + key copy blocks. (Testing the hook and skeleton, not writing 7 full sites.)

---

### V1 — "The Sovereign Stack" (positioning-first)

**Hero:**
> **Your data never leaves your building.**
> Private AI systems, web platforms, and digital products — engineered in-house, deployed on your infrastructure, owned by you.

**Structure:** Problem (cloud AI = your data on someone else's servers) → The alternative (sovereign deployments) → Three services → Proof (reference deployments) → Contact.

**Key blocks:**
- "Most AI tools require you to hand over your documents to a US cloud. For legal firms, medical practices, and engineering bureaus in the EU, that's not a feature — it's a liability."
- Services framed as: Private AI Systems / Web Platforms / Brand-to-Product.
- Proof strip: "2 production RAG deployments · 30 years in visual & web technology · EU-based."

**Bet:** Lead with the sharpest differentiator (data sovereignty) even though it narrows the front door.

---

### V2 — "30 Years, One Principle" (narrative-first)

**Hero:**
> **From prepress to private AI — 30 years of building things that work.**
> I've reinvented my craft five times. The principle never changed: understand the system, then build it properly.

**Structure:** Story arc (prepress → 360° photography → web → AI) → What I do today → Selected work → Contact.

**Key blocks:**
- Timeline: 1996 prepress → 60M Google Maps views → 1,000+ business listings → production AI systems.
- "Every technology wave, I was there early — not chasing hype, shipping products."

**Bet:** The career arc itself is the credibility. Nobody can copy a biography.

---

### V3 — "Proof, Not Promises" (portfolio-first)

**Hero:**
> **See the work. Then let's talk.**

(One line. Immediately below: four case study cards — RAG system, tennis club SaaS, cosmetics brand + e-commerce, legal AI.)

**Structure:** Work grid on the homepage → each case: problem / solution / stack / outcome → Services page derived from patterns in the work → short About → Contact.

**Key blocks:**
- Case card format: "Historical archive RAG — 100k+ WWII documents, hybrid search, multi-tenant. FastAPI · Qdrant · BGE-M3."
- Services page opens: "Everything below exists because a client needed it and I built it."

**Bet:** For a referred visitor, evidence beats copywriting. Zero persuasion, maximum proof.

---

### V4 — "The Full-Stack Partner" (services-first, classic consultancy)

**Hero:**
> **One partner. From brand to backend.**
> Strategy, design, development, and AI systems — without the agency overhead.

**Structure:** Three service pillars with icons → How we work (process steps) → Selected work → About → Contact.

**Key blocks:**
- Classic three-column services layout with "Learn more" links.
- Process: Discovery → Proposal → Phased delivery → Support.

**Bet:** Familiar consultancy pattern; visitors know how to read it instantly.

---

### V5 — "Systems That Stay Yours" (outcome/ownership-first)

**Hero:**
> **I build systems you own — not subscriptions you rent.**
> Web platforms, private AI, and digital products that run on your terms, on your infrastructure, under your control.

**Structure:** Ownership manifesto (3 short paragraphs) → Services as three ownership promises → Work → Working with me → Contact.

**Key blocks:**
- "When the project ends, you hold the keys: the code, the data, the deployment. No vendor lock-in, no monthly ransom."
- Each service ends with "What you own when we're done: …"

**Bet:** Ownership/control is the common thread across dev, AI, and hybrid work — one frame unifies everything.

---

### V6 — "The Anti-Agency" (contrarian differentiation)

**Hero:**
> **No account managers. No handoffs. No juniors learning on your budget.**
> You work directly with the person who designs it, builds it, and ships it.

**Structure:** Differentiation block (agency vs. me, side by side) → Services → Work → "Who this is for / not for" → Contact.

**Key blocks:**
- Comparison: "Agency: your brief passes through 4 people. Here: you explain it once, to the person writing the code."
- "Not for you if: you need a 20-person team tomorrow, or a vendor to blame."

**Bet:** Referred visitors have usually been burned by agencies; naming that pain builds instant trust.

---

### V7 — "Founder to Founder" (direct one-pager)

**Hero:**
> **Hi, I'm Radosav. I build web platforms and private AI systems.**
> Someone probably sent you here. Here's what you need to know.

**Structure:** Single long page: intro → 3 services in one paragraph each → 4 work links → "email me" → done. No navigation.

**Key blocks:**
- "I won't waste your time with marketing copy. Scroll for 60 seconds and you'll know if we should talk."

**Bet:** Radical brevity as respect for the visitor's time.

## PART 2 — THE JUDGES

| Judge | What they punish | What they reward |
|---|---|---|
| **Skeptical CFO** | Fluff, unverifiable claims, risk | Concrete proof, risk reduction, clear engagement model |
| **Midnight Founder** (distracted, on phone) | Walls of text, unclear headline | 10-second clarity, skimmable structure |
| **Competitor** | Anything they could copy by Friday | Defensible positioning, unique assets |
| **Ideal Customer** (EU firm, referred) | Salesiness, vagueness about their problem | Feeling understood, validation of the referral |
| **Conversion Copywriter** | Weak hooks, buried CTAs | Hook strength, narrative flow, clear next step |

### Why the merge wins
No single version satisfied all five judges — each optimized for one reader. The pattern in the scores:

1. **V5's ownership frame** is the only frame that unifies dev + AI + hybrid services (Competitor & Copywriter both flagged this) → becomes the **master narrative**.
2. **V1's sovereignty hook** scored highest with the actual buyer (Customer: 9) → becomes the **Private AI service page lead** and a strong second beat on Home.
3. **V6's anti-agency block** ("you work with the person who builds it") had the best emotional resonance (Founder: 9) and defensibility (Competitor: 8) → becomes the **differentiation section** on Home, softened for CFO tolerance.
4. **V3's case format** (problem/solution/stack/outcome) was the CFO's favorite proof structure → becomes the **Work page template**.
5. **V2 compressed to 4 lines** → About page credibility strip.
6. **V7's direct voice** → the overall tone of everything.

## Post-tournament notes (risks & limits)

- **CFO's unanswered question from V6:** the bus-factor concern ("one person = risk"). The "I bring in specialists / single point of accountability" line partially answers it; consider a short continuity note on the Services page for enterprise-flavored leads.
- **"Monthly ransom"** was cut from the final — memorable but board-hostile. "Not subscriptions you rent" carries the same idea safely.
- **Outcome numbers:** V3's CFO gave 8/10 *conditional on numbers*. Before Phase 4, collect 1 hard number per case study (documents indexed, load time, revenue processed, members served). This is the single highest-leverage content task.
- **SR version:** translate after EN is final; the "Own what you build" frame needs a proper Serbian equivalent, not a literal translation — flag for later.
